Full Site Clean-Up

Clearing that matches your programme

Land and block clearing around Brisbane, Ipswich and Logan ranges from tight infill lots to larger parcels before civil drainage or housing stages. We map which trees stay for erosion control or council conditions, which can be felled outright, and where chip and logs need to leave site because there is nowhere to stockpile on a busy kerb.

Our crew works with your survey pegs and site manager so services are not damaged and sediment controls stay intact. Where koala habitat or local laws apply, we follow the paperwork you supply and keep removal within the approved footprint.

Mulch can go to approved reuse centres or stay on site for temporary track stabilisation — we agree that before machines roll in so truck movements match gate times and noise limits.

What is involved

Typical passes include isolating utilities, directional felling where space allows, and sectional dismantling next to boundaries. Brush is mulched; merchantable timber is cut to length for removal or on-site storage; stumps are ground or extracted depending on your civil design. We can leave a low stump table for surveyors if that helps the next trade.

Dust and track damage are managed with water carts where required and by limiting repeated runs over saturated ground during SEQ wet weeks.

What to expect on the day

Expect a documented pre-start chat, spotter communication while machines move, and clearly marked no-go zones around services. We stage loads to minimise time on the road during school peak if your site demands it, and we sweep the footpath before knock-off so neighbours are not walking through chip.

Why use arborists for clearing

  • Trees near assets are dismantled with rigging rather than guessed drops.
  • Compliance paperwork is easier when removals align with approved plans.
  • One contractor can hand over a pad ready for your earthworks crew.

Common questions

Do I need a permit to clear native vegetation in Queensland?

Many clearing activities require assessment under state vegetation maps or local government planning schemes. We do not issue permits, but we will only proceed once you show the relevant approval or exemption so both sides stay on the right side of the law.

Can you work alongside civil earthworks?

Yes — we coordinate lifts and trench routes so excavators are not waiting on hanging trees. Sequencing matters on tight Logan or Ipswich stages where dewatering and drainage runs sit close to boundary vegetation.

What happens to the mulch from a full block clear?

Mulch can be trucked to recycling yards, spread as temporary erosion control, or stockpiled for later landscaping depending on your development conditions. We quote haulage and tip fees separately where they apply.
